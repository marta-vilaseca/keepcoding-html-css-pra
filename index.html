Bilbo Baggins is a Hobbit of the Shire, the main protagonist of The Hobbit and a secondary character in The Lord of the Rings.

The wizard Gandalf suggested to Thorin Oakenshield and his Company of Dwarves that they hire Bilbo Baggins to be their burglar in the Quest of Erebor, and they all later fought together in the Battle of the Five Armies.

Bilbo was also one of the bearers of the One Ring, and the first to voluntarily give it up, although with some difficulty. He wrote many of his adventures in a book he called There and Back Again.

Bilbo adopted his second-cousin-once-removed Frodo Baggins to be his heir after his parents, Drogo and Primula, died in an accident.

He is the first hobbit to become famous in the world at large, and one of the few to set foot in the Undying Lands.
